How to pick the right AWS Services for your Application

Published by: Abdul Wahab on Dec 01, 2020

The Move to Cloud

As more organizations start making their Public Cloud Adoption journey, chances are they will either be migrating existing APIs/applications to the cloud, and even building new ones. If you are looking into leveraging AWS, you want to make sure that you choose the right tech stack to build your APIs/applications.

AWS is like a huge candy store, with ~100+ available services to choose from for building applications. As a responsible developer, you want to make sure you identify the pros and cons of each particular AWS service you consider (aspects like: integration with other applications/platforms, availability, pricing, future growth/scalability options, permissions, etc.).

These are aspects you should address as early as possible, before drawing out any sort of architectural design or programming. Addressing these concerns initially will help enable you to have highly-reliable APIs/applications in the future that will help grow your business.

Step 0: Know your Business and Customer Requirements

This step is of utmost importance while considering AWS. Before you pick any AWS tech stack, you need to know your business and customer requirements. Some important considerations include:

  • The Business flows your API/service will support (example: submit requests, update/store information, etc.)
  • The Customer group - who executes those business streams and where from? (example: clients, different frameworks/applications)
  • Knowing what is the recurrence of every flow? (example 2000 requests per every hour, 100 assignments everyday, etc.)
  • Understanding the business impacts via quantifiable metrics, if for example, any of these flows quit working or malfunction for: 1 moment, 5 minutes, 60 minutes, 8 hours, 24 hours?
Having the answers to those points will give you the correct setting to settle on potential AWS services.
